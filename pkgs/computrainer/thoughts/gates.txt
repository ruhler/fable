Gates
=====

I want to change the set of gates. What we have today:
* none, rail, sink, source, zero, one, switch, select, dup

Motivations for the change:
* switch + select is awkward and unintuitive.
* I think we can streamline things more.

Proposal:
1. none - still needed, mostly for compact representations of models in text form.
You can't move a car into the gate. It otherwise don't do anything.

2. rail - basic movement. Like today. Passive. You push cars through to the
output direction.

3. sink - removal of cars. Like today. All it does is get rid of the car on
top of it, if any.

4. source - a common gate for source, zero, one, parameterized by car type.
If empty, it populates the gate with the car. Has a direction like a rail to
push cars through. No synchronization, so if you have two back to back
trains, you won't get the source gate's car added in between the trains. Use a
'gate' gate if that's what you need.

5. dup - duplication. As today. Entirely passive. You can push car through,
you have to push out both outputs.

6. switch - single gate where front of car controls which way the train goes
If the front is 0, go to the 0 out. Otherwise go to the 1 out. Synchronization
of some kind is needed to keep track of when to reset the current output
direction (after the end of the train has gone through).

7. gate - synchronization primitive - gate opens when the other side clears,
closes after one train gets through. Otherwise has a direction you can push
through.

8. split - peel a single car off the front of a train.
The car goes to its output, the rest of the train goes through. Needs state to
track when the car ends and when the next train starts.

How to make the equivalent of today's zero or one gate: put a source zero or
source one after a gate gate.

How to make an equivalent switch/select:

* Split the select input, the train output goes to a sink, the head goes to
  the data input track just after a gate gate.
* Send input data through the gate gate, it picks up the head from select, go
  through the new switch gate, then split the output to drop the head into a
  sink and output the rest of the train.

Is there some general way to think of gates / synchronization? Some common
things we have to support:
* output direction(s)
* creation/removal of cars
* aligment on train edge.

It feels like that alignment on train edge is the hard thing. How does it

gate:
* starts down. goes up when output dir gets occupied. Goes back down after
  caboose leaves.

switch:
* starts 'down'. goes up when first car arrives to assign location. Goes back
  down after caboose leaves.

split:
* starts 'down'. goes up when first car is split up. Goes back down after
  caboose leaves.

In all cases, the gate starts blocking the train based on some local event.
Then you push the rest of the train through, then goes back to the other
state.

  idle/waiting -> busy/processing -> caboose -> idle/waiting
