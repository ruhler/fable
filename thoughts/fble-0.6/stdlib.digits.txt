Digits
======
I have Int literals that define local BinLit@, OctLit@, intLit@, HexLit@. I
don't want to bring /Core/Digits% over to the standard library beyond that.

My last use case for /Core/Digits% is md5. Let's see how they are used and
what I can do to replace them.

How they are used:
* Hash@ is defined as 32 Hex@ digits.
* BitN values and bit wise operations defined in terms of Bit@.
* We have literals to construct the BitN values.

It's pretty core right now to the md5 implementation. But it's also pretty
messy the way md5 is implemented.

Rounds operate on 32 bit words. The padder has some 64 bit arithmetic.

Options:
1. Move /Core/Digits into md5 package. Worry about things later.
2. Implement bitwise operations in md5 as Int@ + truncate.
3. Implement bitwise operations in md5 as List@<Bool@>.

The obvious thing to do right now is move Digits into md5 and just worry about
things later. Sounds like a plan to me.
