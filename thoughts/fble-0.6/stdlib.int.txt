Stdlib Int
==========

Existing:
* Int@, 0 through 10
* Incr, Decr, Add, Sub, Neg, Mul, Exp2, Abs
* Eq, Show, Map, Ord, etc.
* Lt, Gt, Le, Ge, IsZero, IsNegative
* Max, Min
* Lit
* Div, Sqrt

C99:
* int, fixed width
* limits on values.
* literals
* operators: * / % + - ++ -- ! ~ >> << ^ | &
*  <=, ==, != >=, <, >

I'm providing unbounded integers rather than fixed width, so the bitwise
operations don't seem appropriate for me.

Haskell:
* Integer
* Enum, Eq, Ord, Show, Read, etc.
* Num is: + * abs signum fromInteger negate

Go:
* literals: decimal, octal, hex, binary, 
* all your typical operators.

My vote is basically what I have, just reorganize and abstract things a bit.

* Int@ private type.
* Literals: decimal, hex, binary, octal
* Operators: Incr, Decr, Add, Sub, Neg, Mul, Div, Mod, Sqrt, Exp2
* Equality: Eq, Ne, Gt, Ge, Lt, Le
* Abs, Max, Min, 
* IsZero, IsNegative, IsPositive, or just SigNum?
* Read, Show
* I don't think we need 0 through 10 anymore.
* No need for exposing IntP@.
