MVP 1
=====

Cars: Engine, Passenger, Cargo
 - Engine needed at front of a train to drive it.
 - Passenger car carries passengers.
 - Cargo car carries goods.

Rails: Move, Stop
 - Move moves the train in the given direction.
 - Stop causes the train to stop and load/unload cars based on what the cars
   are next to. Once loading/unloading is done, the train proceeds again in
   the given direction.

Goods: Refreshed Passenger, Tired Passenger, Good

Factory: House, Factory, Export
 - House turns a tired passenger into a refreshed passenger. It starts with 1
   refreshed passenger. Max occupancy of 1.
 - Factory turns a refreshed passenger into a tired passenger and a good.
 - Export takes away goods from trains.

There's a lot here to get started on. This is a plenty complicated MVP.

Game state:
* Placed rails.
* Unplaced rails.
* Trains - Location, sequence and contents of cars.
* Factories - Location, supply/demand, average supply/demand rates.

Let's measure time in units of ticks. Perhaps keep track of a running average

I have trains, I have rails. How do we model factories? What are the actions
we want?

Trains can move and change their contents. What happens to a factory?

A factory has a capacity of goods and a collection of goods in the warehouse.

Let's focus on MVP1 factories and figure out the rates.

House: Takes some number of time for a person to go from leisure back to
labor. Max capacity of 1 labor and 1 leisure. Can only convert leisure if
there is space for labor?

Factory: Would be nice to have multiple works allowed at once. Let's say... 3
workers per factory max? After some number of time, 1 labor turns into 1
leisure and 1 item. But it's time from when that labor arrives. So we maybe
want to track, for each unit of labor, how long it has been there?

Maybe for each tick, we see how many of each item is in the warehouse, and we
count that towards contribution of a pending thing? So we have a max capacity,
a current capacity, and a list for progress?

Or, maybe we have an intermediate good, or a future good? As soon as we have
enough inputs for a good, form a future good, freeing up space to put more
things. The future good counts down as long as needed, then turns into an
actual good, assuming there is space?

Probably we need to keep inputs and outputs separate, to make sure trains
don't move things the wrong direction.

We also need to keep track of how many items were loaded and unloaded from a
factory in some given reasonable span of time. So probably a list of
load/unload times that, when they get old enough, we forget about them.

How about this for a factory:
* You have as of yet unused inputs in a warehouse somewhere.
* You have completed outputs in a warehouse somewhere.
* You have some number of in process production goods.

Behavior is:
* If an in process good is complete and there is output space, move the good
  to the output.
* If there is space in the process queue and inputs are available, move the
  inputs to in process.

An item stays in process for a given number of ticks.

Max capacity for a factory is: all the inputs plus all the outputs plus
everything that's in process.

For this MVP, let's assume factories are square, so we don't have to keep
track of orientation. House can be 1x1. Let's say factory and export are both
3x3. Or we can play with sizes. Just keep them square.

How to 'tick' a model:
* For each train
 - If it is at a stop, for each car load/unload as able.
 - Otherwise, or if no loading/unloading was done:
 - Move the train based on the direction of the rail, assuming the target of
   the direction is unoccupied rail.
* For each factory
 - Move inputs to processes.
 - Update process timestamps.
 - Move processes to outputs.
 - Update metrics.

How do we want to order these operations? In theory it would be modeling
things happening concurrently. Points of conflict:
* A train moves into where another train is leaving.
* A train loads/unloads to a factory input/output that is consumed/produced.
* Factory internal conflicts on inputs to processes to outputs.
* Multiple trains loading/unloading from the same factory.
* Multiple trains trying to move into the same space.

These conflicts are real, and I suspect there are cases where they have a
fairly significant impact on performance. For MVP1 it doesn't matter. Some
possible approaches for resolving conflicts:
* Pick an order for trains and factories to be processed.
 - Random, or arbitrary, or clearly prescribed.

Let's not worry too much for now. Pick an arbitrary order: trains in whatever
order we have them listed, then factories in whatever order we have them
listed.

To advance a train, we want (Train@, Model@) { Model@; }

To advance a train, we need to update that train and the factories in a model.
To advance a factory, we just need to update that individual factory.

The most tedious part is loading/unloading from a train car. When we see a
train is at a stop, we iterate through each of its cars. For each car, we look
at the spaces around it. We need to find the list of all adjacent factories.

Ignoring ordering for now, go through each factory, in order: transfer 1 good
from the train car to the factory, if possible. Let's say, for mvp1, we have
max transfer of 1 good of each type per car for each tick.

Which means I need:
* Train -> car coordinate
* Car coordinate -> adjacent coordinates
* Adjacent coordinate -> factory contents and reference

I need a way to identify a factory. Options:
* Give each factory a unique id.
* Refer to the factory by its origin coordinates.

Simplest approach to start is use a factory's coordinates as its id.

* Train -> car coordinate
 - Straight forward. Following the directions from the train coordinates, car
   by car.

* Car coordinate -> adjacent coordinates
 - We can generate all 4 easily enough. 2 will presumably be track.

* Adjacent coordinate -> factory coordinate
 - Depends on the size of the factory. Or, I can maintain a separate map, from
   coordinate to factory coordinate. Aka, from coordinate to factory id.

Any advantage to using a coordinate instead of a unique id to identify a
factory? With a unique id, we look up id of a factory by coordinate. We look
up contents of a factory by its unique id. We modify contents by its unique
id. I don't see any advantage to using a coordinate.
