How about this:

Define jank as any time we get a tick request where the upcoming tick event is
in the past. And we can see how far it is in the past to measure the magnitude
of that jank.

Switch to reporting a jank histogram. I can maintain a vector of int, where
index i counts the number of jank occurrences where the magnitude of the jank
was between 2^i and 2^(i+1) ms. That gives us full visibility into the
problem. I suppose we should count number of frames with 0 jank too, for
comparison.

We can also count total and max jank cost and report those.

I can also introduce a notion of a running jank cost. Let's say every tick we
do:
  running_jank_cost = running_jank_cost / 2 + current_jank_cost;

So we have some decay. Bigger jank stays longer. But also back to back jank
stays longer.
