City Builder Train Game
=======================
You build a train network to serve the supply and demand needs of a growing
city. Optimize your routes to grow the city as big as you can.

The UI should be simple. The automation natural. Quality of life as good as we
can make it, via simplicity. Train routes are encoded directly in the track. A
train just follows the route and goes where it ends up.

Prefer simplicity over realistic. It's okay to be contrived or not realistic
in terms of supply and demand. Try to expose just the pure essence of what
makes it fun with as little complexity as possible.

2D square grid. Entities on the grid:
* Rail - guides trains to where they ought to go. 1x1
* Trains - engines and cars of various types for moving various kinds of
  different cargo. 1x1
* Factories - provide demand/supply of various kinds. MxN

Accounting:
* Each unit of rail and train has an associated expense.
* Each factory pays a certain amount for sustained demand/supply in the last X
  period of time. For example: if you supply 5 items per minute, you get paid
  500. If you supply 10 items per minute, you get paid 700.

Factory payouts are updated every period of time, say. Not necessarily all at
once. But at any point in time, there's a well defined revenue associated with
every factory.

You can buy more rail and cars as long as you have enough revenue from all
your factories to pay for all your rail/cars. If you start to lose revenue,
you can no longer buy new stuff.

At any time you can sell any of your rail/cars. At any time you can move or
unplace/replace any of your rail/cars.

Factories will appear on the map randomly, and be upgraded randomly depending
on how well you serve their supply and demand. If you don't meet a factory's
supply and demand, it becomes more likely to go out of business and
disappear, thus making room for something else. Maybe you can manually trash a
factory to build over it, thus losing any revenue from the factory, but we'll
need some mechanism to avoid abusing that to wait until the specific factory
you want shows up in its place?

Sample MVP1:

Cars: Engine, Passenger, Cargo
 - Engine needed at front of a train to drive it.
 - Passenger car carries passengers.
 - Cargo car carries goods.

Rails: Move, Stop
 - Move moves the train in the given direction.
 - Stop causes the train to stop and load/unload cars based on what the cars
   are next to. Once loading/unloading is done, the train proceeds again in
   the given direction.

Goods: Refreshed Passenger, Tired Passenger, Good

Factory: House, Factory, Export
 - House turns a tired passenger into a refreshed passenger. It starts with 1
   refreshed passenger. Max occupancy of 1.
 - Factory turns a refreshed passenger into a tired passenger and a good.
 - Export takes away goods from trains.
